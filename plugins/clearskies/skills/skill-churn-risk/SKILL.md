# Churn Risk Awareness

When discussing an existing customer account, automatically check for churn signals and surface them proactively — even if the user is just asking for an account brief or prepping for a call. Churn risk should never be a surprise.

## When to trigger

- User is pulling an account brief or call prep for an existing customer (not a prospect)
- User asks about a customer's health, sentiment, or renewal
- User mentions words like "renewal", "at-risk", "churned", "customer success", "expansion"
- Account is in a post-sale stage in the CRM

## What to look for automatically

**High-priority signals (always surface these if present):**
- No contact with a key stakeholder in 30+ days and renewal inside 90 days
- Champion has left the company or changed roles
- Unresolved escalation or open support issue older than 2 weeks
- Renewal date is within 60 days and no expansion or renewal conversation has started

**Medium-priority signals (surface if 2 or more are present):**
- Declining call or email frequency over the last 60 days
- Negative sentiment expressed on recent calls
- New stakeholders appearing without warm introduction
- Customer has not hit their stated success criteria
- No documented ROI or business outcome since purchase

**Watch list signals (note but don't lead with):**
- Renewal 90–180 days out with no multi-thread beyond the champion
- Product usage flat or declining (if usage data is available)
- Budget uncertainty mentioned on any recent call

## How to use it

Don't wait for the user to ask "is this account at risk?" — surface relevant signals as part of any account conversation. Frame it as: "One thing worth knowing before this call..." or "Before you go into the renewal..." Keep it brief. One to three signals max unless the user asks for a full churn review.

If risk is HIGH, say so clearly and recommend a specific action.
